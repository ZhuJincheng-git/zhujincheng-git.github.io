# The Great Failure

## Discourse Power

Discourse power brings credibility, and credibility brings discourse power.

## Measurability

Keep things measurable, and collectively construct control over the system.

## Concealing Truth

Truth that should be made explicit is deliberately hidden, mildly damaging trust. Concealment ensures safety, borrowing from future credibility.

## Dual Narrative

A dual narrative of success and consolation brings a sober confusion, allowing ambiguity to excuse failure.

## Misjudging Risk

Narrative bias leads to false optimism.

## Acquired Silence

Widespread acquired silence slows information flow, turning silence into a rational survival strategy.

## Local Stubbornness

Localized stubbornness persists, lagging behind and creating sharp contrasts that remain unresolved, relying on the system's self-regulation while nurturing new stubborn parts.

## Misattribution

Using local behavioral issues to evade systemic design problems.

## Obedience Testing

Continuing to apply traditional experiences that are no longer reliable, turning them into obedience tests.

## Ensuring Non‑Exit

Cultivating an inertial environment that raises the cost of exit.

## Active Proliferation

Protecting extreme cases without resolving them, allowing the system to proliferate on its own.

---

*If failure is not defined, it does not occur. Ambiguity is where bargaining power lies.*
